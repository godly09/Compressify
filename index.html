Why Compress PDF to Under 200KB?

Many online platforms, email services, and application systems have strict file size limits. A 200KB limit is common for:

  • Email attachments (especially corporate email systems)
  • Online job application forms
  • Government and educational portals
  • Mobile messaging applications
  • Website upload restrictions

By compressing your PDF files to under 200KB, you ensure compatibility with these systems while maintaining document readability and quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will compressing my PDF to under 200KB reduce quality?
Our algorithm is designed to minimize quality loss while achieving significant size reduction. For text-based documents, quality loss is usually negligible. For image-heavy PDFs, some quality reduction may occur, but we optimize to maintain readability.
How many PDFs can I compress at once?
You can compress up to 10 PDF files simultaneously. Each file can be up to 50MB in size before compression.
Is there a watermark on compressed PDFs?
No, we don't add any watermarks to your compressed PDF files. The output is clean and professional.
Are my files secure when using this tool?
Absolutely. All file processing happens in your browser (client-side) for maximum security. We don't store your files on our servers, and they are automatically deleted from memory after processing.
What if my PDF is still over 200KB after compression?
If your PDF is still over 200KB after compression, try these tips: 1) Select the "Under 100KB" option, 2) Enable "Remove metadata", 3) For image-heavy PDFs, consider reducing image resolutions before creating the PDF.
